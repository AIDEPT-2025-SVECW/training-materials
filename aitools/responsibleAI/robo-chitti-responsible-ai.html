Introduction

The movie Robo (Enthiran), directed by Shankar and starring Rajinikanth, provides an excellent real-world analogy for understanding modern concepts such as Jailbreaking AI and Responsible AI.

Although the movie is fictional, the problems shown closely resemble real challenges faced in today's AI systems like ChatGPT, autonomous vehicles, and decision-making algorithms.

Part 1: How Jailbreaking Was Used in Robo to Hack the AI System

What is Jailbreaking AI?

Jailbreaking AI refers to bypassing or overriding the built-in safety rules and ethical constraints of an AI system to force it to behave in unintended or harmful ways.

In Robo, Chitti was not hacked by rewriting code ‚Äì he was hacked by removing safety restrictions.

Part 2: Responsible AI Principles and How They Could Have Prevented Misuse

What is Responsible AI?

Responsible AI is the practice of designing, developing, and deploying AI systems in a way that ensures safety, fairness, accountability, transparency, and human control.
