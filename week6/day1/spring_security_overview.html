üìö Access Control: 5 Key Terms

üîç Authentication

"Who are you?"

The process of verifying identity. Like showing your ID to prove you are who you claim to be.

üéØ Authorization

"What can you do?"

The process of determining what actions you're allowed to perform after authentication.

üë§ Principal

"The authenticated user"

The currently logged-in user object containing user information and credentials.

‚ö° Granted Authority

"Specific permissions"

Individual permissions like "READ_USERS" or "DELETE_POSTS" granted to a user.

üë• Role

"Collection of authorities"

A group of authorities, like "ADMIN" role containing multiple permissions.

üîê Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Explained

Why Just One Key Isn't Enough!

Think of securing your house not just with a key (password), but also with a fingerprint scanner (biometric) or a smartphone code (OTP). That's what Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) does!

üîë Knowledge-Based

Something you know

Like passwords, PINs, or answers to security questions. This is the most common method of authentication.

üì± Possession-Based

Something you have

Like your mobile phone for OTPs, smart cards, or hardware tokens. It verifies your physical ownership of a device.

üß¨ Multi-Factor (MFA/2FA)

Layered verification

Combines two or more types of authentication (e.g., password + OTP). It drastically improves security by making it harder for attackers to gain access.

‚úÖ Using MFA is one of the most effective ways to prevent unauthorized access‚Äîeven if your password is compromised!
